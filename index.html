Mexican wolf facts

1 features

The Mexican wolf is a species similar in shape and size to a medium-sized dog; his body is slim and solidly built; its head is narrow and with a long and angular skull, it has large, straight ears with rounded tips.

2 about the mexican wolf

The Mexican wolf is the smallest subspecies of the 30 subspecies of North America. Its habitat can be found in various places, from desert and semi-arid areas to forests. In the late 1970s, the Mexican gray wolf (Canis lupus baileyi) was declared endangered. At that time it was estimated that there were barely 50 specimens in all of Mexico.

habitat and diet

Diet

their diet is based on deer, sheep, antelope, hares and rodents. The presence of the Mexican wolf in ecosystems is key to its balance, since being a carnivorous predator it regulates the populations of its prey

Habitat

Mexican gray wolves are found in a variety of habitats, including mountain forests and the deserts of Chihuahua and Sonora.

important facts

Behaviour

It is a social animal and forms herds of family groups with a partner that reproduces for life, called an alpha pair, and their descendants.

reproduction

the species can reproduce only once a year, has a gestation period of 60 to 63 days and has a litter of 3 to 9 young

threats and conservation

The reduction of its prey and the hunting of the species brought it to the brink of extinction, to avoid its extinction, Mexico and the United States made a drastic decision: they captured 5 wolves they found and subjected them to a captive breeding program
